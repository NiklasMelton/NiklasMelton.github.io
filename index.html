Bio

Niklas M. Melton is a machine learning researcher and data scientist. He received his B.S. in Aerospace Engineering from the Missouri University of Science and Technology in 2017 and his Ph.D. in Computer Science from the Missouri University of Science and Technology in 2026.

His research focuses on cluster validity indices, incremental and lifelong learning, explainable AI, and Adaptive Resonance Theory, with applications to streaming data, interpretable evaluation, and the analysis of learning systems over time. He has contributed to peer-reviewed work in clustering, lifelong learning, and computational intelligence, as well as software tools including the Adaptive Resonance Library.

In 2025, he received the Outstanding Paper Award for “An Extensive Analysis of Match-Tracking Methods for ARTMAP” at the IEEE Symposium on Computational Intelligence in Health and Medicine (CIHM), as well as the Best Presentation Award for “The Need for More Nuanced Metrics of Catastrophic Forgetting” at the 12th International Conference on Soft Computing & Machine Intelligence (ISCMI). He also has seven years of professional experience as a data scientist in the finance, healthcare, and SaaS industries.
